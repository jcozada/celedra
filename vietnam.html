Things to Do in Vietnam

  • Ha Long Bay

    Ha Long Bay, a World Heritage Site in Vietnam, is a breathtaking natural wonder with thousands of limestone karsts and isles rising from emerald waters.

    Cruises

    Overnight Cruise: Budget-friendly to luxurious, includes meals and cabins.

    Day Cruise: Short visits to caves and kayaking.

    Water Activities

    Kayaking, Swimming, Bamboo Boat Tours.

    Exploring Islands & Caves

    Sung Sot Cave (Surprise Cave) One of the largest and most stunning caves in Ha Long Bay, featuring impressive stalactites and stalagmites.

    Thien Cung Cave (Heavenly Palace Cave): Another beautiful cave adorned with intricate formations and colorful lighting.

    Ti Top Island: Climb to the viewpoint at the top for panoramic views of Ha Long Bay. The island also has a small beach for swimming.

    Cat Ba Island: The largest island in the Cat Ba archipelago, offering hiking, beaches, and the entrance to Cat Ba National Park. You can take a ferry or speedboat here from Ha Long City or some cruise routes include it.

    Monkey Island (Cat Dua Island): Home to playful monkeys and offers a nice beach for swimming and kayaking.

    Other Activities

    Floating Villages, Rock Climbing, Seaplane Tours, Sunset Watching, Ha Long City.

  • Ha Long Bay

    Hoi An, a charming ancient trading port in Vietnam and a World Heritage Site, offers a delightful blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Here are some of the top things to do in Hoi An:

    Exploring the Ancient Town

    Wander through the Old Town: Get lost in the narrow, lantern-lit streets lined with colorful merchant houses, tailor shops, art galleries, and cafes. This is best done on foot.

    Visit the Japanese Covered Bridge: An iconic symbol of Hoi An, this beautiful bridge was built by the Japanese community in the 17th century.

    Explore the Quan Cong Temple: A vibrant and colorful Chinese temple dedicated to the god of war.

    See the Tran Family Chapel: Another historical house with beautiful architecture and ancestral shrines.

    Beyond the Old Town:

    Relax on An Bang Beach: A short taxi or bike ride from the Old Town, An Bang Beach offers sandy shores, turquoise waters, and plenty of seafood restaurants.

    Cycle through the Rice Paddies: Rent a bicycle and explore the scenic countryside surrounding Hoi An, passing by lush rice paddies, water buffalo, and local villages.

    Visit the My Son Sanctuary: A World Heritage Site about an hour's drive from Hoi An, these ancient Cham ruins offer a glimpse into Vietnam's pre-Vietnamese history.

    Take a Boat Trip to the Cham Islands (Cu Lao Cham): A group of islands offering beautiful beaches, snorkeling, diving, and fresh seafood.

    Food and Drink:

    Indulge in Local Specialties: Don't miss trying Cao Lau (unique noodles), White Rose dumplings, Banh Xeo (crispy pancake), and fresh seafood.

    Enjoy Street Food: Hoi An's streets are alive with delicious and affordable street food options.

  • Hue

    The former imperial capital of Vietnam and a World Heritage Site, offers a rich tapestry of history, culture, and serene landscapes. Here are some of the top things to do in Hue:

    Exploring the Imperial Citadel (Kinh Thanh):

    Wander through the Citadel: Explore the vast complex that was once the seat of the Nguyen Dynasty emperors. Discover its impressive gates, courtyards, and pavilions.

    Visit the Forbidden Purple City (Tu Cam Thanh): The inner sanctum of the Citadel, once reserved for the emperor and his concubines. Though heavily damaged during the war, restoration efforts are ongoing.

    See the Noon Gate (Ngo Mon): The main southern gate and a magnificent example of Vietnamese imperial architecture.

    Admire the Nine Holy Cannons (Cuu Vi Than Cong): Located near the Citadel's walls, these massive cannons symbolize the power and defense of the Nguyen Dynasty.

    Exploring Pagodas and Monasteries:

    Thien Mu Pagoda: An iconic symbol of Hue, this seven-tiered pagoda sits on the banks of the Perfume River and offers stunning views.

    Huyen Tran Princess Temple: A complex of temples and pagodas dedicated to a revered historical figure.

    Tu Dam Pagoda: A significant Buddhist temple with a serene atmosphere and beautiful gardens.

    Other Activities

    Take a Dragon Boat Trip, Walk Across Trang Tien Bridge, Try Hue Cuisine, Explore the Old French Quarter, Visit Dong Ba Market, Lang Co Beach.

  • Hanoi

    The the bustling and historic capital of Vietnam, offers a captivating blend of ancient traditions and modern energy. Here are some of the top things to do in Hanoi:

    Exploring the Old Quarter (Hoan Kiem District):

    Visit Hoan Kiem Lake and Ngoc Son Temple: The serene heart of Hanoi, legendarily home to a golden turtle. Walk across the iconic red Huc Bridge to reach the temple on a small island.

    Explore Dong Xuan Market: Hanoi's largest covered market, offering a wide array of goods from fresh produce to clothing and souvenirs.

    Wander the narrow streets: Get lost in the maze of bustling streets, each traditionally specializing in a particular trade (e.g., Silk Street, Paper Street, Tin Street).

    Historical and Cultural Sites:

    Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum and Museum: Pay respects to Vietnam's revolutionary leader at his mausoleum and learn about his life at the nearby museum.

    Temple of Literature (Van Mieu-Quoc Tu Giam): Vietnam's first university, a beautiful complex of courtyards, temples, and gardens.

    National Museum of Vietnamese History: Discover the rich history and cultural heritage of Vietnam through fascinating exhibits.

    Thang Long Imperial Citadel: A World Heritage Site showcasing the historical layers of Hanoi's imperial past.

    One Pillar Pagoda (Chua Mot Cot): A unique and iconic pagoda built on a single pillar in the middle of a lotus pond.

    Other Activities

    Visit West Lake (Ho Tay), Try their Egg Coffee, Visit Buddhist temples and shrines.

Vietnamese Food

  • Bun Cha

    Grilled pork with vermicelli noodles, fresh herbs, and a dipping sauce – a must-try Hanoi specialty.

  • Pho

    Hanoi is the birthplace of this iconic noodle soup. Try various stalls and restaurants.

  • Banh Mi

    Delicious Vietnamese baguette sandwiches with various fillings.

  • Egg Coffee (Ca Phe Trung)

    A unique and rich Hanoi coffee experience.

  • Goi Cuon

    Fresh spring rolls filled with rice paper, lettuce, herbs, and vermicelli noodles.

  • Banh Xeo

    A crispy, savory pancake made from rice flour batter, often stuffed with shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts.

  • Com Tam

    Broken rice served with grilled pork, fried egg, and pickled vegetables.
